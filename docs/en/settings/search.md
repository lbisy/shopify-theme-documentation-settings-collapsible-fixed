# Search

The **Search** setting controls predictive search. Predictive results appear while customers type, helping them find products and related content more quickly.

## Open the Search settings

Go to **Shopify admin > Online Store > Themes**, click **Customize**, then open **Theme settings > Search**.

![Search settings](/images/en/settings/search.svg)

## Enable predictive search

When enabled, matching suggestions appear before the customer submits a complete query. Depending on the theme and store content, results can include products and other relevant content.

Benefits include:

- Less typing.
- Help confirming product names or spelling.
- Direct access to a match before opening the full results page.
- Faster navigation in stores with large catalogs.

Predictive search is recommended when the store has many products with clear names and customers often search for specific items.

When disabled, no live suggestions appear. Customers submit their query and review the complete results page. This can suit small catalogs, a deliberately simple interface, or stores where predictions are not useful. Disabling predictions does not remove regular search.

## Improve predictive results

- Use clear, accurate product titles.
- Include terms customers commonly use.
- Avoid indistinguishable duplicate names.
- Publish relevant products to the Online Store sales channel.
- Keep images, prices, and availability current.

Recent product-title or status changes may take a short time to appear in storefront results.

## Save and test

Click **Save**, then:

1. Open search from the header.
2. Type part of a full product name.
3. Try common categories, brands, and features.
4. Confirm suggestions match the query.
5. Open a suggestion and verify its destination.
6. Submit a query and verify the full results page.
7. Try a query with no matches and check the empty state.

Test desktop and mobile for overflow, long-title wrapping, image and price alignment, closing behavior, and keyboard use with arrow keys, `Tab`, and `Enter`. Use several popular, similar, and multilingual product terms rather than testing only one query.
